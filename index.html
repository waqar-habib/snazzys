Project Brief: Snazzy's Food Truck

Introduction

Snazzy’s Food Truck is a Contemporary Desi Street Eatery - an up-and-coming place to dine out in a casual setting with friends and family. Snazzy’s will offer a variety of items from the South Asian culture – featuring Indian/Pakistani foods fused with North American dishes to create a modern spin on delicious South Asian dishes. Whether its butter chicken pizza or Foodles, a spin off Lo Mein, Snazzy’s got it all. Not to mention, Chai – black tea leaves brewed with milk and sugar, will definitely put a smile on your face.

Business Needs

Being in the United States for about 15 years, I noticed that there were a lot of homeless people on the streets, no matter what city I visited. A lot of people would usually shrug and say, “oh they are just drug addicts! We don’t want to give our money to them, only so they can buy more drugs!” However, one does not realize that even they are human beings and aside from procuring drugs, they still need to eat throughout the day. Now I’m not saying that they are doing good to humanity by being drug addicts, but we still cannot eliminate them from society completely. This is the reason why I decided to open Snazzy’s so that the poor and the homeless don’t have to line up in soup kitchens to get their bland meals of the day – instead they could make their way to our food truck and get real, hot meals whenever they get hungry. Maybe some of those who turn up at our kitchen can be ambitious and we could onboard them as cooks to change their lives…this just maybe possible as well.

If Snazzy’s is successful, what will be the impact on the business?

If the business model behind Snazzy’s is a success, Snazzy’s might be able to upgrade to a full-size brick and mortar store all the while helping out the needy, one meal at a time.

Revenue Model

The revenue model behind Snazzy’s is simple - whenever any one of us stops by and buys a meal for themselves, they can opt to buy one extra meal for the needy. Additionally, the Snazzy’s team is willing to contribute some of our profit in order to fund those meals as well, so we don’t have to depend on our clientele entirely.

Example Use Case


Use case for meal ordering website:

Individuals can use the Snazzy’s website to place food orders directly to the food truck. When the user places an order, the website describes the business model in a language that they understand and appeals to their empathetic side. Next, they are politely requested to add a meal for the needy. They have the option to completely skip the process and then are prompted to pay through the website or pay when they pick up their food. Once that is confirmed, the food truck will receive a request through their system. The food will then be prepared, packaged, and handed to the individual upon arrival. In this case, the website must be able to receive orders, process payments, and communicate with the restaurant electronically.

System:

Food ordering website

Primary actor:

Customer ordering a meal

Scenario:

The user navigates to https://www.snazzys.com and browses through the menu options. Once they select their item, they are taken to the next screen where they can add another meal for the needy (or skip that page). If they choose to add a meal, they are taken to a page where there are prepaid meal packets available to purchase and their price is listed transparently. They can then select the item(s) place an order through the website. The user pays online or verifies they will pay in person upon arrival. The order is sent from the app to the restaurant’s internal system. The restaurant worker receives and processes the electronic order.

Stakeholders & Users

The internal stakeholders of Snazzy’s will be myself and my family who will help run the kitchen. In addition to my family, the suppliers for the equipment inside the food truck, including but not limited to kitchen supplies will also play an important part in the idea being successful. Finally, my extended family, who has generously offered to become investors will also be a part of this dream of mine. As for the target users, our customers and patrons will be the ones who will externally fund our project by visiting and dining out at our food truck.


Target User: John, a hungry customer

Usage: John uses the online ordering system at Snazzys.com to order food so he can pick up the food in person at the truck. Motivations & Goals: John is using Snazzys.com to order their next meal. At the same time, they also desire to help the community by donating a meal to the needy. John is a loving and thoughtful gentleman who wants to end hunger one meal at a time.

Project Requirements

The project requires the following high-level requirements:

  • Landing page is the main page where the user is directed to upon navigating to the website.
  • About Us page which lists the revenue model and the story behind the idea.
  • Menu page with pictures of items that the user can browse through.
  • Online ordering page where the user can order their meals internally.
  • Contact Us page where the user can reach out to the Snazzy’s team.

    • Success measures

    Success for Snazzy’s is not defined by how much profit we make – instead the success is measured in terms of client satisfaction, patrons that purchase and donate a meal as well as the needy who come to the food truck and request a meal. A good key performance indicator (KPI) for Snazzy’s will be a counter of how many meals we were able to provide to the needy. Of course the profitability of the business will depend on how many clientele we are able to drive towards the business by properly marketing the idea to local foodbanks, churches & mosques as well as local government.

    • Risks

    A major risk is that the business does not take off as intended. If the main target users refrain from donating a meal, Snazzy’s won’t be able to support the business model by itself. The limited budget is another risk because supporting a fully electronic ordering system takes a lot of money that needs to be invested upfront before any sales can be measured.
